What is Pilates?

The pilates method is a mind-body centering technique that emphasises the importance of central core stability.

The essence of physiotherapy aims to create a balanced aligned body, pilates compliments this perfectly. Matwork pilates consists of exercises performed on the mat that are designed to promote core stability, flexibility, endurance, postural and body awareness and to correct muscle imbalances.

Pilates is based on the following eight principles:

  1. Breathing - focus on the breathing cycle is essential in pilates and is one of the most difficult principles for beginners.
  2. Concerntration - Pilates is a work-out for the mind and body.
  3. Control - Movement control is key when working against gravity in matwork exercise.
  4. Centering - In pilates your centre is your powerhouse.
  5. Precision - this is a long-term goal in pilates which develops with practice.
  6. Flow - pilates exercises are performed in even, continuously flowing movements
  7. Intergrated isolation - pilates builds mind-body awareness, regular pilates allows one to recognise incorrect movement patterns, isolate and correct them.
  8. Routine - as with any exercise therapy, repition lead to greater skill and benefits.
